## Proposed Optimal Order (User Journey Logic):

**A facility manager lands on the site. What do they want to know?**

1. **Hero** — Immediate value proposition (keep)
2. **Services** — "What do you actually do?" (MOVE UP — #1 question)
3. **Sectors** — "Do you serve my industry?" (MOVE UP — #2 question)
4. **About** — "Why are you different?" (credibility after they see relevance)
5. **Technology** — "How do you deliver?" (proof of intelligence)
6. **Process** — "What's the onboarding like?" (reduces friction)
7. **After Clean™** — "What extras do you offer?" (upsell/differentiator)
8. **Testimonials** — Social proof (builds trust before contact)
9. **CTA** — Contact form (conversion point)
10. **Footer**

**REMOVED from homepage (moved to dedicated pages):**
- Founder → /about page (too personal for homepage, slows the buyer journey)
- Pillars → Merge into /about page or /technology page (redundant with About section)
- Sustainability → /sustainability page (important but not primary decision factor)

## Dedicated Pages to Create:

1. **/about** — Company story + Founder + Pillars framework + Team values
2. **/services** — All 10 services with individual expandable sections (SEO goldmine)
3. **/sectors** — All 9 sectors with detail pages (SEO goldmine)
4. **/technology** — EagleWing Command™ + After Clean™ + Sustainability
5. **/process** — Detailed onboarding process
6. **/contact** — Standalone contact page
7. **/blog** — Already exists

## Rationale:
- B2B buyers want: relevance → credibility → proof → action
- Services & Sectors answer "Can you help ME?" — must come first
- Technology & Process answer "HOW will you help?" — comes after interest
- Testimonials provide final push before conversion
- Founder/Pillars/Sustainability are "nice to know" — better on dedicated pages
- Dedicated pages = more SEO landing pages = more organic traffic
